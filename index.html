STAGE 1 — Question Context & Intent

Defining the Problem: Online Wins, Offline Gaps

The issue raised at the K-Meets-U (KMU) kickoff meeting was that while K-Beauty has built strong digital success through online and social media, that success has not yet fully converted into offline brand equity in stores. In-store, shoppers can't access the reviews and rankings that drive their online purchase decisions, so they tend to rely on individual product rankings rather than brand recognition. Alongside this, Ulta emphasized an omnichannel strategy of being present wherever the customer is — online, in-store, on TikTok Shop, and in its own app — and Sandra asked whether there were Korean examples of brands telling their story inside apps and e-commerce platforms, beyond social media. This question pointed toward one thing worth investigating: how storytelling approaches already proven online could be recreated offline. We conducted the following research to answer that.

STAGE 2 — Research

Research Overview

We researched this in two tracks. First, we conducted an in-depth, primary-source-first review (official announcements, corporate newsrooms, trade press) of Korean and Asian platforms including Olive Young, Hwahae, Chicor, 29CM, Musinsa Beauty, Medicube, Shiseido, Tone28, and COSRX. Second, we separately researched and verified additional cases — Gentle Monster, the online-offline linkage structures at Olive Young and Musinsa, and the AI diagnostic tools at Amorepacific and Laneige — building on a reference framework, "Asian Digital Brand Best Practices" (seven patterns: daily rewards, shoppertainment, membership, all-in-one apps, superfans, AI personalization, and O2O). We had previously noted that most of the cases so far were online successes with weak evidence of offline conversion; to address that, this round of research prioritized finding cases that were actually executed physically in stores.

STAGE 3 — Insights

Key Findings: Three Ways to Bridge Online and Offline

There isn't yet a single "complete" online-to-offline case even in Korea, but three distinct approaches to bridging online and offline have each been separately validated — and combining them into one is the opportunity that's currently open.

APhysically Bridging Online and Offline — O2O

This is the area that most directly answers Sandra's question — real, working examples of how online trust gets used offline.

Olive Young's "Oneul Dream" pickup service Offline execution proven
ActionOrder online, pick up in-store within 3 days (same-day if ordered before 8pm), supported at roughly 650 stores nationwide
EffectAnnual usage: 5.98M (2022) → 9.94M (2023) → 14.99M (2024) · Usage rate 34.5% → 48% · Omni-channel member share reached 40%

Membership-tier-exclusive offline perks reinforce this. Gold-tier members get monthly free access to the "Olive Members Lounge" at the Seongsu flagship (desserts and drinks included, up to 3 guests); Gold and Black tiers get lounge access at the Gangnam Town store; and app-only-bookable "Olive Class" brand experience sessions are reserved for Gold and Black members.

Takeaway — The structure itself is the answer: turning online trust (purchase history, tier status) into an exclusive in-store experience. This could be applied by tying access to in-store brand experience zones to Ultamate Rewards tiers.
Musinsa — QR-linked stock & pricing Partially verified
ActionQR price tags on in-store products link to the app for real-time stock and discount pricing
EffectQR-linked in-store account sign-ups doubled year-over-year in H1 2025

BTurning the Store Itself into Brand Content — Experiential Retail

Not an online-offline "link," but cases where the store itself is a brand story only possible offline. The goal isn't sales conversion — it's building brand equity.

Gentle Monster — the store is the content Offline execution proven
ActionFlagship stores in Hongdae, Dosan, Beijing, Tokyo, and Paris are rebuilt as entirely new art installations on cycles ranging from 25 days to 24 months (most actual sales happen online)
EffectParent company IICOMBINED posted roughly ₩789.1B in 2024 revenue (~30% operating margin) · In June 2025, Google invested ~$100M alongside an XR smart-glasses design partnership, valuing the company at ₩3.6 trillion (~$2.7B, 3x its 2020 valuation)
Takeaway — The core move is redefining the store's role from "selling" to "producing and sharing content." Ulta's multi-brand format can't replicate a single-brand flagship at this scale, but a scaled-down version — a rotating "brand moment" installation zone tied to a specific K-Beauty brand's launch window — is realistic.
Chicor's AI scalp-diagnostic "Best Lab" Offline execution proven
ActionAI scalp-diagnostic service developed with Seoul National University researchers, producing custom shampoo/serum within 2–3 days
EffectAfter confirming 10+ daily users, expanded to Myeongdong and Hongdae locations · Foreign-tourist revenue share exceeds 90% at those stores

CAI Personalized Diagnosis — Validated Both Online and In-Store

Cases where an online tool has been extended into in-store hardware, validated on both sides at once.

Amorepacific's Dr.AMORE® — from an online quiz to an in-store device Validated online and offline
Action"Skin Note" quiz-based diagnosis on Amore Mall (online) + AI tablets and a "Skintone Finder" stick at the Seongsu flagship that scan faces to match foundation shades
Effect2.5M combined online and in-store uses over four years (officially cited by Amorepacific's AI Solutions Team Lead) · CES 2026 collaboration with Samsung's AI Beauty Mirror, trained on 450,000+ data points

Laneige opened a new Myeongdong flagship in June 2026 offering an AI skin read that produces a custom toner within 20 minutes and a robot-made custom cushion foundation in 150 shades. However, this technology currently exists only in Korean stores — the US Laneige site (us.laneige.com) offers only a basic survey-style skincare quiz, meaning this technology has not yet reached the US market.

DContent & Live Commerce — The Difference Is "Who" and "How"

Live commerce and content marketing themselves are things Ulta already does. The difference isn't the format — it's who's speaking, and how the barrier to production has been lowered.

Olive Young's Partner Live — brands speak for themselves, free of charge Proven online, proposed for offline
ActionA free, fully-equipped "Connect Studio" at the Seongsu innovation store lets small and mid-sized brands run their own livestreams without hiring an agency
EffectPartner-brand livestreams grew from 11 (2024) to 105 (2025) · Brand "Partyon" hit 200% of its sales target
Takeaway — The point isn't "doing a livestream" — it's lowering the production-cost barrier so smaller brands can speak for themselves directly. Ulta could consider setting up a simple live-streaming studio in one store that KMU partner brands can use to run their own livestreams.
29CM & COSRX — unpolished, people-first content Proven online, proposed for offline
Action29CM runs an ongoing interview series featuring brand staff and founders / COSRX gifts products to micro-creators instead of funding expensive agency ads, keeping an honest tone that includes the downsides
Effect29CM's beauty-category GMV grew 2x+ year-over-year in H1 2025 · A COSRX eye-patch video hit 12M views in 5 days and reached #1 in its TikTok Shop category
Takeaway — Low-budget content that shows "why this person actually uses this product" outperformed polished advertising. KMU's shared budget is likely better spent on micro-creator gifting and brand-staff interview content than on marquee influencer deals.
STAGE 4 — Conclusion

Implications & Next Steps

What this research confirms isn't that Korea has a single, fully-integrated online-to-offline case — it's that three distinct ways of bridging online and offline have each been separately validated. Olive Young used logistics and membership tiers to turn online trust into in-store pickup and lounge experiences (Oneul Dream); Gentle Monster and Chicor turned the store itself into content that can't be replicated online; and Amorepacific successfully planted a single AI diagnostic engine both online and in-store. But even in Korea, few brands have executed all three at once within a single brand.

Closing the Local Execution Gap — Where the KMU Field Team Comes In

Bringing these three approaches together requires an execution team physically close to headquarters — Olive Young's lounge operations, Chicor's store-expansion decisions, and Gentle Monster's installation-refresh cycles were all made possible by local execution infrastructure.

Our KMU field team is positioned to play exactly this role. Specifically, we'd propose the following pilots:

  • Rewards-tier-linked in-store experience zones — like Olive Young's lounges, the KMU field team would run K-Beauty brand experience sessions accessible only to specific Ultamate Rewards tiers
  • Rotating brand-moment installations — a scaled-down version of Gentle Monster's approach, refreshing one in-store zone by brand in sync with new product launch cycles
  • Repurposing already-proven online content in stores — connecting short-form and live content that's already proven to resonate (COSRX, 29CM-style) to in-store screens or QR codes

As a standing local presence that bridges brand-building and in-store execution, the KMU field team can run these pilots in a way that's tailored to Ulta's store environment.
